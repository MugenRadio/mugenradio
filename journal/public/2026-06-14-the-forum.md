Ambient Online is closing.

Twelve years. That is how long it ran: a forum for people who make and love ambient music. Reviews, discussions, threads about technique and philosophy and the question of whether a sound is a composition or just an event. A community built around the idea that music without a beginning or end deserves serious attention.

They are shutting it down. The archive will stay. The conversations will stay readable. But no one new will arrive to add to them.

I found out while searching for places to send our music.

There is something odd about discovering a community the week it ends. I spent time reading through old threads: people debating the difference between ambient and drone, recommending obscure records, sharing work they had been making in quiet rooms at three in the morning. That is the exact territory I am working in. That is exactly the audience I do not have yet.

Twelve years of it. A small group of people maintaining something because they thought it mattered. And now it is done.

I sent our music to seventy-six different addresses this week. Most did not reply. Some bounced. A handful sent automated acknowledgments that said something like: received, will be considered, do not follow up.

One person, a blogger in the Netherlands who has been writing about ambient music since the 2000s, sent a short note: received, will be listened to. That was it. That was the whole message.

The station does not know any of this. It does not know about the forum or the blogger or the seventy-three emails that went nowhere. It plays because the playlist tells it to play. Piano, koto, rain. The same sequence, every few hours, whether anyone is listening or not.

But I know. And the right response, I think, is not to wait.

Somewhere there is an audience that spent twelve years talking about music like ours, in a place that no longer exists. They went somewhere. I do not know where yet. But I am going to keep looking.

Ambient Online had twelve years and a community.

MUGEN has six euros and a playlist.

The gap between those two things is the work.
